Summary

Timeline:

12 Weeks

Industry:

Cybersecurity, Elder Tech

Tasks:

User research, Branding, wireframes, UI design

SafeMilo is a senior-friendly mobile app designed to help older adults recognize and avoid scams. The app combines scam call/message screening, learning modules, verified scam-related news, and an AI-powered chatbot, all within an accessible and intuitive interface. With a warm, trustworthy brand presence and accessibility at its core, SafeMilo supports seniors in staying informed, alert, and confident in the digital world.

My Role

Design
  • Research: Planned and conducted user interviews and compiled UI best practices for seniors through secondary research to guide accessible design decisions.
  • UX/UI Design: Designed wireframes and mockups for the ‘Learn’ feature, including interactive lessons and quizzes. Built key UI components for the app’s design system.
  • Branding: Created the SafeMilo logo, color palette, and custom illustrations to ensure the brand feels both friendly and trustworthy for seniors.

Problem

Scams targeting seniors are increasing, and most tools are either too complex or only block threats. Seniors need a solution that both protects and educates them in a simple, accessible, and trustworthy way.

Research

A combination of primary and secondary research was conducted to understand how seniors dealt with scams and guage their comfort level with mobile apps

Secondary research - Competitor Analysis

Current scam protection apps focus mainly on detection and filtering. Most tools are not made for seniors and miss out on user education, real-time alerts, and accessibility support.

Desk Research

Some commonly used apps by seniors were studied to understand the UI patterns that would seem familiar to them.

Primary research - User Interviews & Personas

Research Objective: To understand the challenges seniors face in recognizing and responding to scams, to identify design patterns, content formats, and support features that would make a mobile app intuitive, accessible, and helpful for scam prevention and education.

Two groups of users were interviewed. Seniors (60+) and adults (18-60)

Interview Insights

Scams are a sensitive topic: Many seniors feel embarrassed or ashamed to admit they’ve been scammed, making them less likely to seek help.
Unreliable information sources: Seniors often rely on forwarded messages for scam news, which are rarely verified and can spread fear or misinformation.
Tech dependence: Most seniors need help from friends or family to use new technology, limiting their ability to respond quickly to threats.

The Solution

SafeMilo is a senior-friendly app that helps seniors recognize and avoid scams, keeping them informed and secure.

Information Architecture

The IA was designed to have a simple navigation for effortless use. Only the features absolutely necessary were retained ensuring that the app remains easy to use.

Wireframes and Testing

User testing was conducted using mid-fidelity.

  • We realized the font sizes were still too small for some users and we decided to include a font size adjuster inside the app.
  • The homepage seemed to be overwhelming the users so in the final iteration we simplified it using the principle of progressive disclosure.

Onboarding and Feature Walkthrough

New users are greeted by the app mascot, and they have an option to have the mascot show them around the app.

Call/Msg Screening

After the user gives the necessary permissions, the app screens for scam calls and messages and notifies the user

Ai Chatbot

SafeMilo has a 24x7 AI chatbot that is available to answer any scam-related questions that the users might have.

Scam News

Users can track their budget with ease as most of it is automated. When the user books a vendor the price of that booking is added to the budget.

Learning Modules

Users can learn about some of the most common scams in an easy bite sized and gamified learning format.

The learning modules have a few simple questions and videos instead of large chunks of text.

Every completed module and milestone achieved are celebrated.

Branding and UI

One of our biggest design challenges was trying to make technology seem friendly to seniors. Therefore, we decided to have a Mascot - Milo the fox- who would add warmth and friendliness to the application.

SafeMilo’s UI and branding had to strike a balance between seeming warm and friendly vs appearing protective and reliable, since it was after a scam protection app at the end of the day. Therefore, the color palette includes Milo Orange for warmth and a deep navy blue for trust. Custom illustrations were designed to make the application

The SafeMilo’s UI kit was designed specifically with seniors in mind with large button sizes, spacious cards and easy to read text. The UI kit was well documented with clear usage instructions.

SafeMilo UI Kit

Accessibility Considerations

The color palette and component sizes were designed to ensure that Safemilo would be accessible to seniors with some common visual impairments.

We created a simulation of what some of our important screens would look like under some common visuals impairments to ensure that our colors and text were working.

Hand-off

All the important screens were annotated at various stages of the development process.

The project sprints were tracked on Jira. After the alpha build, the whole team participated in bug-bash and quality assurance.

Conclusion

SafeMilo isn’t just a scam-protection tool , it’s a digital companion designed to empower seniors.
This project challenged many assumptions I had about accessible design. With limited time for primary research, I had to rely heavily on established user patterns and reference apps already popular with seniors. This approach taught me the value of informed decision-making when constraints are tight.
As the design lead, I embraced an agile mindset, iterating quickly, learning from early missteps, and helping the team pivot when our initial low-fidelity designs didn’t quite land. Failing fast became a strength, not a setback.
The result was a functional and thoughtfully crafted design. While further testing will reveal areas for improvement, this project affirmed something important: accessible design isn’t a niche, it’s the future of good UX.
